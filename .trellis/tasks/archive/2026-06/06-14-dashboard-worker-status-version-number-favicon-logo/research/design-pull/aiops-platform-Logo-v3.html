Identity · logo · v3

The unfinished line

v1 drew a tuning fork. v2 drew a dial. Different costumes, same idea — a thin-stroke pictogram wearing a borrowed music metaphor, with an accent pinhead that died at 16px. v3 stops drawing pictures. The system's voice is a console log that learned typography — so the mark is exactly that: the italic Newsreader a, mid-line, the console's block caret still blinking after it. Not a picture of operations — a line still being written.

v1 · v2 — the rut, named
v1 v2
  • ✕One idea, two costumes. Both were stroke pictograms + a satellite accent dot + a music metaphor that needed a paragraph of caption. Iterating the costume again would not leave the rut.
  • ✕Stroke marks thin out. A 5px stroke in a 64-unit frame turns spindly at 24px and furry at 16. Letterforms are the one class of shape designed to survive every size — that is what type is.
  • ✕The accent was decoration. A pinhead whose only job was to be colored — first thing to vanish, nothing lost when it did. In v3 the accent is a cursor: solid, rectangular, and it means something — the stream is live.

  1. 1

    The glyph — the editorial voice

    Newsreader italic at 500 — the exact recipe the design system prescribes for its brand mark. It says a for aiops, set the way the dashboard sets its numbers.

  2. 2

    The caret — the live session

    The console's block cursor, in the accent. It blinks at 1s steps(2) — the event log's own caretBlink — whenever the surface is live. The system is mid-sentence; the work is not done.

  3. 3

    The shared baseline — the platform

    Letter and cursor stand on one line. The baseline is the platform: the single invariant under everything that runs — cmd/worker, the poll tick, the budget.
